# Key points about iOS 26.4

Read through the terms of service and found a few things that stand out/

## Silent system updates you can't fully disable

**Section 1c.** Even if you turn off automatic OS updates, Apple reserves the right to silently push "system files" — fonts, language models, voice assets, peripheral firmware — whenever you use certain features. There is no full opt-out. You're told it happens, but the toggle doesn't cover everything.

## Broad data consent baked into feature use

**Section 4.** Using Location Services, Siri, or Dictation implicitly consents to your data being sent to Apple. The consent is somewhat buried — it's framed as "details will be provided when you turn on these features" rather than surfaced upfront. The act of enabling the feature *is* the consent.

## $250 liability cap

**Section 8.** Apple's total liability to you for all damages is capped at $250 USD, regardless of what goes wrong. For a device that costs $1,000+, this is notably lopsided. A bricked phone, lost data, a bad update — the most you can recover is $250.

## Jailbreaking voids Apple Pay

**Apple Pay Section 6.** Modifying your own hardware can get you permanently cut off from Apple Pay and Wallet. This is fairly aggressive given that device modification is legal in many jurisdictions. Your phone, their payment rails.

## HIPAA disclaimer

**Section 10b.** Apple explicitly says: don't use Apple devices or services to handle protected health information. That's a significant carve-out given how many health apps live on iOS and how heavily Apple markets health features.

## California law governs everything

**Section 11.** Unless you're in the UK, California law applies to disputes globally. This can limit your local consumer protections depending on where you live. Japan's jurisdictional supplement does carve out intentional misconduct liability, but most users are governed by Cupertino's home turf.

## The AI outputs disclaimer

**Section 5h.** There's a clause burying responsibility for AI-generated content accuracy onto you. You're told to evaluate AI outputs yourself before relying on them. Broad and self-serving given that Apple Intelligence is now woven into the OS.

---

None of these are unusual for a major tech EULA, but the **$250 liability cap** and the **silent system file update carve-out** are the two most worth knowing about. The AI disclaimer will probably age into relevance as Apple Intelligence takes on more responsibility in the interface.
